Some of my favorite classes I have taken so far are (in no strict order):

Object-Oriented Design

This class was focused on the principles of "good" software design, and the patterns that can help you achieve good design in your program.

We learned about 5 principles that software engineers have come to a consensus on as being pillars of good design.

Design patterns are solutions to problems threatening good design that are recurrent– ones that pop up over and over again in every object-oriented programmer's tasks.

I found that trying to make my software cooperate with the design rules (principles) we learned to be a really interesting challenge, almost like a puzzle. I found that whenever I worked with private fields and tried to envision a solutions to problems regarding information hiding, I would personify the units of my program, allowing me to envision the solutions and patterns more easily. What this gave me was a new way to look at programs– as networks of individual units. This is a really good way to look at your programs when the thing you are trying to design is large and involved and will require a lot of components. Or at least, that's how object-oriented design sees it.

Matrix Methods in Data Analysis and Machine Learning

We started with more theoretical things whose relation to data analysis I didn't really understand. Then, we went over two types of regression– linear regression for the prediction of real labels, and logistic regression for binary labels. We learned about different loss functions you can use. We learned about the standard ones like RSS and cross-entropy, as well as terms you can add to your loss function to give it properties you'd like. For instance, we learned that for feature selection we can add the L1 norm of the parameters to the RSS. We can add the L2 norm for a less strong feature prioritization.
